# Enterprise Control Layers

## Purpose

This section explains why durable enterprise AI value may reside in control layers rather than only in visible experience layers.

## Working argument

Enterprise AI is not only a competition over intelligence or interface quality. It is also a competition over control points: the layers that constrain, route, interpret, validate, escalate, or govern action inside real workflows.

## Experience layers

Experience layers are the visible surfaces people interact with directly, such as assistants, dashboards, workflow front ends, and chat interfaces.

## Control layers

Control layers are the less visible but often more durable parts of the operating environment. They include permissions, thresholds, escalation logic, interpretation rules, decision rights, and accountability structures.

## Four control functions

1. Observability: what changed?
2. Governance: what is allowed?
3. Interpretation: does this matter?
4. Decision rights: who decides what happens next?
