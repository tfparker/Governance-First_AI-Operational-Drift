# Bounded Operational Trust

## Working definition

Bounded operational trust is the degree to which a system can be relied on within a defined operating scope to surface, qualify, or support action credibly, without generating unmanageable noise, hidden logic, or ambiguous responsibility.

## Key attributes

- bounded scope
- explainable qualification
- low-noise behavior
- challengeability
- preserved human accountability
- repeatable credibility under use

## Why it matters

A system may be technically capable but operationally untrusted if it overwhelms users, obscures reasoning, creates unclear responsibility, or produces signals that are not worth attention.
