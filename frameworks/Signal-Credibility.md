# Signal Credibility

## Working definition

Signal credibility is the degree to which a surfaced signal is believed to be worth attention because it is qualified, explainable, proportionate, and contextually meaningful.

## Core distinction

Detection is not the same as credible attention.

A system can detect something and still fail to produce a signal that operators trust or know how to act on.

## Why low noise matters

False positives are not just user-experience defects. They create operational waste, escalation fatigue, and eventual disregard for the system.
