# Operational Drift, Signal Credibility, and Bounded AI Governance

Copyright © 2026 Tripp Parker

This repository is a governance-first writing and research project on enterprise AI control layers, operational drift, signal credibility, and bounded interpretation systems.

The central premise is that durable enterprise AI value may depend less on broad automation claims and more on whether systems can support credible interpretation under constraint.
In complex operating environments, the question is not only what a system can detect, generate, or automate. 
The harder question is whether it can help people determine what deserves attention, why it deserves attention, and who remains accountable for acting on it.

## Working thesis

Enterprise AI systems should not be evaluated only by visible capability or interface sophistication. In many operating contexts, the more durable layer may be the control point that helps constrain, qualify, interpret, escalate, or govern action.

This project explores that thesis through a narrower lens:

- operational drift
- signal credibility
- governed interpretation
- low-noise escalation
- bounded operational trust
- human-reviewed decision support

## What this project is

This is a public-facing thesis and framework repository. It is intended to organize writing, definitions, and structured notes around governance-first AI and operational interpretation.

It is not presented as a deployed product, client-validated system, or production implementation.

## What this project is not

This repository does not claim that AI systems should own broad enterprise decision-making. It does not position bounded interpretation as a replacement for human accountability, systems of record, reporting controls, or operational review.

The project is intentionally skeptical of broad agent, copilot, and autonomous workflow framing when those labels blur accountability or increase operational noise.

## Core concepts

### Operational drift

Operational drift refers to movement away from expected process, data, reporting, control, or workflow behavior. Not every drift signal deserves escalation. The important question is whether the drift is meaningful, credible, and actionable within a defined context.

### Signal credibility

Signal credibility is the degree to which a surfaced signal is trusted as worth attention because it is qualified, proportionate, explainable, and context-aware.

### Bounded operational trust

Bounded operational trust is the degree to which a system can be relied on within a defined operating scope to support interpretation or escalation without creating unmanaged noise, hidden logic, or ambiguous responsibility.

### Governed interpretation

Governed interpretation is the disciplined qualification of signals under defined constraints, thresholds, review expectations, and accountability boundaries.
